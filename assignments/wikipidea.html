Nepal

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Nepal is a landlocked country in South Asia. It is mainly situated in the Himalayas, but also includes parts of the Indo-Gangetic Plain. It borders China to the north and India to the south, east, and west.

Nepal has a diverse geography, including fertile plains, subalpine forested hills, and eight of the world's ten highest mountains, including Mount Everest. Kathmandu is the capital and largest city.

Etymology

The name "Nepal" is first recorded in texts from the Vedic period of the Indian subcontinent. Hinduism was founded in ancient Nepal, the predominant religion of the country.

History

In the middle of the first millennium BC, Gautama Buddha was born in Lumbini in southern Nepal. The Kathmandu Valley was the seat of the ancient Nepal Mandala.

By the 18th century, the Gorkha Kingdom achieved the unification of Nepal. The country was never colonised but served as a buffer state between China and British India.

Geography

Nepal is divided into three principal physiographic belts: the Himalayas, the Hill region, and the Terai. The Himalayas include the highest mountains in the world.

Politics and government

Nepal is a federal parliamentary republic divided into seven provinces. The Constitution of Nepal was adopted in 2015.

Economy

Nepal's economy is developing and largely dependent on agriculture, remittances, tourism, and foreign aid.

Culture

Nepal is multi-ethnic, multi-lingual, multi-religious, and multicultural. Nepali is the official language.

Demographics

Nepal has a population of over 30 million people with diverse ethnic groups and cultures.
